How GeoScore works

A bounded, evidence-first pipeline

Validate the public target, sample at most five pages, build one site profile, run factual checks, calculate coverage/confidence, then stream evidence-backed actions.

HTML, HTTP, DNS and structured data are primary. Optional public providers add provenance-rich evidence and may fail without reducing the score.

Pass weight is divided by known applicable pass + fail weight. Critical, major and minor failures cap high scores; incomplete coverage/confidence can add lower caps. Predicted output has zero weight.

No account is required. Only public targets should be submitted. Cached results reduce duplicate work and do not consume fresh-audit quota.

    Scope and data sources

    GeoScore audits public HTTP(S) sites. Private networks, IP literals, credentials and custom ports are rejected.

    Bounded public fetches

    Site mode samples the homepage, About page and representative internal pages. URL mode audits the target and may read the homepage for context.

    Optional evidence sources

    PageSpeed, CrUX, W3C Nu, Common Crawl, DNS, certificate logs and other public APIs can add evidence. Provider failure is reported as unknown or error, not as a penalty.

    Informational, not professional advice

    Results are SEO/GEO signals, not legal, financial, compliance, penetration-testing or professional security conclusions.

    What GeoScore checks

    Known, applicable factual checks determine readiness scores. Unknown, error and not-applicable states do not reduce them.

    Indexability and discovery

    robots.txt, sitemap discovery, canonical URLs, noindex directives and public fetch status.

    Page semantics

    Titles, descriptions, headings, language, links, images and cross-page consistency.

    Schema fit

    JSON-LD presence is separated from whether a schema type fits the detected site and page type.

    Factual GEO readiness

    Entity and author consistency, direct answers, extractability, claim/source support, statistic provenance and freshness.

    Performance evidence

    PageSpeed lab data and CrUX field data retain source, status and partial-failure details.

    Predicted visibility

    AI simulations are clearly labelled Predicted, carry zero scoring weight and are not presented as real citations.

    SEO and GEO work together

    Search and answer systems both need crawlable pages, clear entities, extractable answers and well-supported claims. GeoScore turns those observable signals into a prioritized repair list without inventing business facts.

    Frequently asked questions

    Short answers about scoring, predictions, privacy and limits.

    Is GeoScore free?+
    Yes. The public service uses fair-use limits for fresh audits. Cached report views do not consume the fresh-audit quota.
    Does GeoScore monitor real AI citations?+
    No. It audits factual SEO/GEO readiness and may show a separate Predicted simulation. It does not claim to observe live citations from third-party answer engines.
    How are scores calculated?+
    Known, applicable pass/fail checks form the raw weighted score. Critical, major and minor failures cap a category at 49, 79 and 94; incomplete coverage or confidence can lower it further. Evidence below the minimum returns no overall score.
    Which language does the report use?+
    The product follows your saved UI language. The report defaults to the audited page language and has its own saved English/Chinese switch.
    What data is stored?+
    GeoScore stores public audit results and operational rate-limit/cache data. Do not submit confidential or private URLs.
    Can it audit localhost or private networks?+
    No. Only public domain hostnames and public HTTP(S) URLs are accepted.
